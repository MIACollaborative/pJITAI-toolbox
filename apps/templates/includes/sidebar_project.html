Why use the pJITAI toolbox?

This toolbox helps you design a reinforcement learning algorithm for a personalizing Just-in-Time Adaptive Intervention (pJITAI) component.

What is Personalization?

Data accruing from an individual may indicate that intervention option A is more effective than intervention option B when the individual is in certain states. A personalizing JITAI will increase the probability of sending intervention option A in these states.

For example, if an individual is more responsive to intervention option A during weekends, the JITAI will send intervention option A more frequently than intervention option B during weekends.

What is a Decision Point?

In a JITAI, a decision point is a point in time where an intervention component decides what type of intervention to deliver, or whether to deliver an intervention at all. For example, an intervention component that promotes walking might include a decision point in the morning before the person goes to work.

What is Personalization (for JITAIs)?

Whereas some JITAI decision rules may be designed with fixed probabilities or deterministic rules (e.g., IF the user did not meet their step goal yesterday THEN deliver a walking suggestion at 9am today), a personalizing JITAI component will employ an algorithm that seeks to learn under what conditions a particular intervention option is more effective for an individual, and to adjust the likelihood of sending that option to optimize overall effectiveness. For example, if a user is more likely to take walks after receiving a reminder on weekends than on weekdays, the algorithm may choose to send a message (rather than send nothing) more often on weekends than on weekdays.

How can I use the pJITAI Toolbox?

The pJITAI Toolbox will walk you through the steps of configuring a personalizing reinforcement learning algorithm. Providing the information requested by the Toolbox will ensure that the algorithm is set up properly for the intervention component you plan to personalize, and that the algorithm is initialized with starting parameters that will allow it to perform reasonably well at the beginning and learn each user’s responsiveness quickly. The current version of the Toolbox assumes that you have prior data from a Micro-randomized Trial (a common way to generate initialization data for a personalizing algorithm), but if you don’t have such data you can come up with estimates from related prior work or simply take your best guess!

How do I start?

Simply click “New Project” from the options on the left. On each of the screens that follow, in addition to general instructions and explanations, you will find examples drawn from the HeartSteps project. A great way to get familiar with the pJITAI toolbox is to create your own “HeartSteps Activity Suggestions” project and walk through the Toolbox entering values from the running example. After you finish that, come back here and start a new project to personalize your own Intervention Component!
