General Settings / Personalization Method

This version of Thompson Sampling uses a Bayesian linear regression to model the relationship between the participant’s state as represented in the RL (e.g., time of day, location, recent activity such as prior hour step count), intervention options, and proximal outcome (i.e., the near-term result of the selected intervention option). The algorithm selects the best intervention option to deliver based on its estimate of the relative effectiveness (a.k.a. the “posterior probability” that this option will lead to best proximal outcome) of the intervention options. This estimate is based on how the participant has responded to each option in similar states in the past, as measured by the proximal outcome.

This version of Thompson Sampling assumes:

  • There are two intervention options.
  • The proximal outcome is continuous.

Note also that this version of Thompson Sampling learns a model for each individual and does not use data from other participants’ data. This approach can provide greater personalization, but takes longer to stabilize (i.e., produce posterior probabilities with high confidence) than approaches that pool data across participants. For more information about Thompson Sampling and other approaches that use pooling (not currently included in the pJITAI Toolbox), see:

  • Trella, A. L., Zhang, K. W., Carpenter, S. M., Elashoff, D., Greer, Z. M., Nahum-Shani, I., Ruenger, D., Shetty, V., & Murphy, S. A. (2024-06). Oralytics Reinforcement Learning Algorithm. https://doi.org/10.48550/arxiv.2406.13127
  • Ghosh, S., Guo, Y., Hung, P.-Y., Coughlin, L., Bonar, E., Nahum-Shani, I., Walton, M., & Murphy, S. (2024-08). MiWaves Reinforcement Learning Algorithm. https://doi.org/10.48550/arxiv.2408.15076
Personalization method

Personalization method refers to the statistical/computational method (i.e., algorithm) that enables the personalization in your intervention. Currently, the pJITAI Toolbox supports the RL algorithm, “Linear Thompson Sampling Contextual Bandit1” Algorithm for personalization, though we plan to add other options in the future.

  1. Thompson Sampling for Contextual Bandits with Linear Payoffs Shipra Agrawal, Navin Goyal Proceedings of the 30th International Conference on Machine Learning, PMLR 28(3):127-135, 2013.
