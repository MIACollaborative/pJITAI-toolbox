General Settings / Personalization Method

This version of Thompson Sampling uses a Bayesian linear regression to model the relationship between the participant’s state as represented in the RL (e.g., time of day, location, recent activity such as prior hour step count), intervention options, and proximal outcome (i.e., the near-term result of the selected intervention option). The algorithm selects the best intervention option to deliver based on its estimate of the relative effectiveness (a.k.a. the “posterior probability” that this option will lead to best proximal outcome) of the intervention options. This estimate is based on how the participant has responded to each option in similar states in the past, as measured by the proximal outcome. This version of Thompson Sampling assumes that

  • There are two intervention options.
  • The proximal outcome must be interval data. For more, see the Wikipedia article on “Level of measurement.”

Note also that this version of Thompson Sampling models each individual separately and does not use other participants’ data. This approach can provide greater personalization, but takes longer to stabilize (i.e., produce posterior probabilities with high confidence) than approaches that pool data across participants. For more information about Thompson Sampling and other approaches that pool data across participants (not currently included in the pJITAI Toolbox), see:

  • Ghosh, S., Guo, Y., Hung, P.-Y., Coughlin, L., Bonar, E., Nahum-Shani, I., Walton, M., & Murphy, S. (2024-08). MiWaves Reinforcement Learning Algorithm. https://doi.org/10.48550/arxiv.2408.15076
