Intervention Settings / Intervention Options

Give names to your two intervention options that will help you remember what they represent in terms of your intervention component.

Intervention Option A - Higher Intensity

The first of the two options that can be selected by your personalizing algorithm should be the one that is more “intense”--both in the sense of burden to the participant and in the sense of expected impact on the outcome. For the typical case of an intervention component that sends a message to the participant, Option A would be the “Send a Message” option.

Intervention Option B - Lower Intensity

The second of the two options should be the option that is lower in intensity, considering both burden and expected outcome. For a push messaging intervention component, this option might be the “No Message” option.

Note:
It's important not to mix up these two options. The system is built around the assumption that Option A is the more "active" or potentially burdensome option and Option B is less active or passive.
HeartSteps Example

For the HeartSteps Activity Suggestions example, Intervention Option A is “Send an activity suggestion message” and Intervention Option B is “Do not send a message.”
